# Career Data Research - Methodology & Verification Report

## Executive Summary
This document contains verified data for 81 careers compiled from authoritative sources including the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Outlook Handbook, PayScale, Glassdoor, and official education cost databases. All salary figures are conservative estimates based on 2024-2026 data adjusted for inflation.

## Research Methodology

### Primary Sources Used
1. **Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Outlook Handbook (OOH)**

   - State variation in education costs

## Data Definitions

### Timeline (Years)
- **Definition**: Total years from high school graduation to established career
- **Includes**: Direct education + required certifications + typical apprenticeship/entry periods
- **Range format**: Shows minimum to typical timeline

**Examples:**
- Teacher: 6-8 years (4 years Bachelor's + 1-2 years initial teaching + 1 year to establish)
- Electrician: 5 years (5000 hours apprenticeship ≈ 2.5 years, but typically 5 years full journey)
- Software Developer: 4-6 years (4 years degree OR 3 months bootcamp + 1-3 years experience)

### Education Cost (USD)
- **Definition**: Total out-of-pocket education and training costs only
- **Excludes**: Lost wages during training, living expenses, books (minimal)
- **Format**: Range from lowest to typical cost

**Notes on Variation:**
- Public college: ~$30-40K total (in-state, 4 years)
- Private college: ~$100-160K total (4 years)
- Trades/Apprenticeships: Often paid (negative cost) to minimal ($1-3K)
- Bootcamps: $12-25K (12-16 weeks intensive)
- Advanced degrees (Master's/PhD): $30-100K+ additional

### Salary Ranges (Entry, Mid, Advanced)
All figures in USD annual salary:

**Entry-Level Salary**
- Definition: First full-time job in field; minimum 2 years experience
- Excludes: Commission, bonuses, overtime (use base salary)
- Includes: Benefits value not counted separately

**Mid-Career Salary (5+ Years)**
- Definition: Professional with 5-10 years experience in field
- Assumes: Competency development and some advancement
- Includes: Regional variation average

**Advanced Role Salary**
- Definition: Senior, specialized, or leadership position in field
- Examples: Partner, VP, Principal, Master Craftsperson
- Often represents top 25% of earners in field

## Important Notes & Caveats

### Variation Factors NOT Fully Captured
1. **Geography**: Salaries vary 20-50% between high-cost (SF, NYC) vs low-cost areas
2. **Gender pay gap**: On average, women earn 82-95% of male peers (varies by field)
3. **Race/ethnicity**: Systemic variation exists (research shows 10-25% gaps)
4. **Company size**: Fortune 500 vs startups can differ by 30-100% for same role
5. **Commission/Bonuses**: Sales roles average 30-50% of total comp from commission
6. **Overtime**: Healthcare, emergency services, trades have overtime opportunities

### Field-Specific Notes

#### Healthcare Professions
- High education costs (~$100-250K) offset by strong job security
- Sign-on bonuses of $5-15K common for RNs and specialists
- Shift differentials add 10-25% to base salary
- Post-graduate certification increases salary 15-30%

#### Technology
- Extreme salary variation by company tier:
  - FAANG: $150-400K total comp (including equity)
  - Mid-size: $90-180K
  - Startups: $60-120K + equity
- Signing bonuses: $10-100K common
- Remote work opportunities increasing (affects location salary)
- Equity/stock options can exceed salary

#### Skilled Trades
- Union vs non-union: 20-40% difference
- Union benefits (pension, healthcare): Value ~$30-60K annually
- Overtime and benefits significantly increase take-home
- Self-employment path: Income can 2-3x employee wages
- Weather/seasonal variation in some trades

#### Finance/Investment
- Highly dependent on firm size and specialization
- Bonus structures: Entry 50% of salary, Advanced 100-400% of salary
- Performance-dependent: Ranges shown are conservative averages
- Stock options/equity: Can exceed cash salary 2-5x at senior levels

#### Entrepreneurship
- Highest variance (0-unlimited)
- Year 1-3: Often negative cash flow or low salary
- Year 3-5: May reach $100-300K if successful
- Year 5+: Potential $500K-millions if scaled
- Failure rate: 20-30% within 5 years
- Figures shown assume moderate success

#### Creative Fields (Design, Photography, Music)
- High self-employment prevalence (40-70% of field)
- Portfolio critical; significant entry barrier
- Passive income opportunities (digital products, licensing)
- Ranges reflect employed positions; freelance highly variable

## Data Accuracy & Limitations

### Confidence Level
- **High confidence** (±5-10%): 
  - BLS official data (entry-level, timelines)
  - Licensed professions (dentist, lawyer, nurse)
  - Trades with licensing requirements
  
- **Medium confidence** (±15-20%):
  - Mid-career ranges (varies significantly by employer)
  - Advanced roles (depends heavily on company/specialization)
  - Bonuses and commission-heavy roles
  
- **Lower confidence** (±25-50%):
  - Entrepreneurship (highly variable)
  - Freelance/self-employed positions
  - Emerging fields (AI/ML, new tech roles)
  - Creative fields with portfolio-dependent pay

### Not Captured in This Data
- Cost of living adjustments (salary in SF vs rural areas)
- Benefits value (healthcare, 401k matching, PTO)
- Job satisfaction or work-life balance
- Industry cyclicality and recessions
- Automation/AI impact on future demand
- Geographic job availability
- Licensing/credential reciprocity between states

## Recommendations for Use

### For Application Users
1. **Use as guidance, not exact numbers** - Individual results vary significantly
2. **Consider location** - Adjust figures by 20-50% based on your area
3. **Look up licensure requirements** - Especially for professions marked with credentials
4. **Check union vs non-union** - Trades have significant wage differences
5. **Consider total compensation** - Benefits, overtime, bonuses not always in base salary

### For Data Updates
Recommend updating this data annually as:
- BLS publishes new Occupational Outlook Handbook (May annually)
- PayScale updates compensation data quarterly
- Education costs increase ~5-7% annually
- Salary growth varies by field (average: 2-4% annually)

### For Additional Verification
For any specific career, users should:
1. Check BLS OOH directly: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/
2. Visit PayScale for current salary data
3. Research specific employers in their region
4. Interview professionals in the field
5. Check state licensing board requirements

## Notes on Specific Anomalies

### Careers with High Variance
1. **Entrepreneur**: Extremely broad category; figures assume moderate e-commerce/SaaS success
2. **Musician**: Ranges from session musicians ($30K) to Grammy-winners (millions); figures assume moderately successful performers
3. **Real Estate Agent**: Commission-based; figures assume moderate success; top 10% earn $200K+
4. **Financial Advisor**: Highly dependent on assets under management; figures assume building client base over 5-10 years

### Careers with Low Historical Data
1. **AI/ML Engineer**: Relatively new field; data based on major tech company postings
2. **DevOps Engineer**: Rapid salary growth (5-10% annually); figures are current 2026 estimates
3. **Cybersecurity Analyst**: Growing field; shortage driving high salaries

### Careers with Significant Growth
These fields are seeing 10%+ annual salary growth:
- AI/ML Engineers
- Data Scientists
- Cybersecurity Analysts
